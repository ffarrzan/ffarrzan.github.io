About

Farzan Karimi-Malekabadi is a computational social psychologist. He recently completed his Ph.D. in Social Psychology at the University of Southern California, building on an earlier background in engineering. His research asks how moral and cultural systems form, vary, and change across people and places — and, increasingly, how large language models reason about those same moral and cultural questions.

Methodologically he works across causal inference, Bayesian and multilevel modeling, psychometrics (CTT/IRT, EFA/CFA/SEM), and NLP, often on large cross-cultural datasets with hundreds of thousands of participants. Alongside his academic work he has applied these methods in industry — as a Quantitative User Research intern at Google and a Research Scientist at Edmentum — designing evaluations for agentic AI products and building pipelines that benchmark and improve LLM-generated content. What ties it together is a commitment to careful measurement: turning fuzzy human constructs into things that can be estimated, tested, and trusted.
