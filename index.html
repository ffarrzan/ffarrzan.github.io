About me

I'm a quantitative researcher (Ph.D., USC) with an extensive methodological toolkit of advanced statistical and computational methods, built on a strong engineering background (B.S. and M.S.).

In my academic research I do:

  • Measuring minds in machines. Using psychological theory and psychometrics to probe what language models represent, how they reason, and whether our evaluations of them measure anything real.
  • Machines and culture, both directions. Generative-agent societies as causal laboratories for social theories that can't be tested on people, and how LLMs are reshaping human language.
  • The ecology of morality. How environments, from pathogens to economic hardship, shape the values people hold and the behavior that follows, across counties, countries, and history.

In industry I do:

  • Quantitative user research. End-to-end studies from scoping to readout: segmentation and journey validation, A/B testing, and assessing user trust and behavior, such as a critical-user-journey segmentation study and an agentic-AI welcome-screen study at Google.
  • AI & LLM evaluation. Designing evaluation protocols and rubrics, running SME calibration studies, and benchmarking model output at scale, including GPT benchmarking on 12K item-skill pairs at Edmentum and agentic-AI research at Google.
  • Psychometrics & measurement. IRT and CTT modeling, reliability and item-quality analysis, and building validated instruments and rubrics.
  • Research data science. Pipelines over large data (Databricks ELT across billions of assessment records), plus dashboards and automated reporting that feed product decisions.

Takes dual-process theory from cognitive psychology and turns it into an alignment target, tuning models along the spectrum from fast, intuitive reasoning to slow, deliberate analysis, and revealing an accuracy-efficiency trade-off between the two.

Machines and culture, both directions

Generative agents as a causal laboratory for social theory, and LLMs as a homogenizing force acting back on human language.

2025

Realistic Threat Perception Drives Intergroup Conflict

Runs a causal experiment that would be impossible with humans: a virtual society of generative agents, with realistic and symbolic threat independently manipulated (and steered at the level of model activations) to trace how conflict escalates over time.

Paper → Code →

The Shrinking Landscape of Linguistic Diversity in the Age of LLMs

Four studies showing the reverse arrow: as people adopt LLMs as writing assistants, their language homogenizes, eroding the stylistic diversity that carries identity, psychology, and culture, and that social science depends on measuring.

Paper → Code & data →

The ecology of morality

Why moral systems vary across places: mapping how environments, from pathogens to hardship, shape values and behavior.

2025

A Value-Based Topography of Climate Change Beliefs and Behaviors

Maps county-level moral values across the United States and shows that the local moral landscape, not just individual attitudes, patterns climate beliefs and green behavior, with direct implications for regionally targeted moral framing.

2024

What Doesn't Kill Us: Harsh Ecologies Beget Difficulty-as-Improvement Norms

Shows across simulation, spatial, historical, and multinational survey evidence that harsher environments (higher mortality, tougher economies and ecologies) breed the sticky cultural belief that unchosen hardship builds character.

Links infectious-disease prevalence to moral concern about purity across U.S. counties, 67 nations, multiple languages, experiments, and a century of history, showing that the behavioral immune system helps shape which values a culture emphasizes.
